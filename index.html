Hello!

I am a Senior Machine Learning Software Engineer at Google, where I focus on advancing the capabilities of machine learning models and AI-driven systems. My primary work has been with the Gemini project, where I led the development and launch of the advanced data analysis feature and seamlessly integrating it with Google Workspace and other tools. I have architected scalable infrastructure for agent orchestration, dynamic prompting and multi-step planning, which enhances model adaptability and flexibility across diverse tasks. My work has also involved improving model quality using prompt engineering, fine-turning and reinforcement learning.

Before Google, I worked on autonomous driving technologies at General Motors, where I helped improve perception and localization for autonomous vehicles. I have a Master's degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington, where my research focused on second-order optimization for neural network training, and I’ve published papers on topics ranging from optimization to autonomous robotics.

In addition to my engineering work, I regularly contribute to the research community by reviewing papers for leading conferences like NeurIPS, ICLR, and IEEE Transactions. My interests span LLMs, deep learning, computer vision, and agentic workflows. I’m continuously seeking new ways to push the boundaries of AI and multimodal understanding to solve complex, real-world problems.
